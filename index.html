Intro

I am Juan Vásquez (he/him), a third-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Colorado Boulder. I am being supervised by Dr. Maria L. Pacheco and Dr. Alexis Palmer.

I am part of BLAST and LECS, where I am currently working on neurosymbolic approaches to NLP. Our goal is to combine symbolic rules and methods with neural architectures to take advantage of the modeling capabilities displayed by both paradigms, and create more robust, efficient and explainable NLP models.

During my master's (in AI), I worked on heteronormative language detection, and hate-speech detection in Mexican Spanish under the supervision of Dr. Gemma Bel Enguix and Dr. Karla Ramírez Pulido.

In my free time I enjoy playing J-RPGs, watching horror films and reading contemporary Latin-American and queer literature.

COOL SOFTWARE

these are some cool tools that i use, and that i think are worth checking out if they fit any of your needs.

→ command-line

  • espanso - one of the coolest pieces of software that I have ever encountered. It allows you to define your own text completions in a toml file. When you type an alias, espanso will replace it with whatever you defined in the toml file. More advance features allow you to execute shell commands by just typing whatever string you define.
  • F2 - a CLI tool for batch renaming files and directories quickly and safely.
  • jq - a CLI json processor.
  • Stirling-PDF - a locally hosted app with tons of features to modify/edit pdfs.
  • typst - a markup-based typsetting language designed to resolve LaTeX ambiguities, and with a simpler syntax.

→ python

  • uv - a super fast dependency, python version(s) installer and manager. it is also a great way to manage your project environments (way better than virtualenv and poetry, IMO.)

→ latex

  • tectonic - a rust-based latex engine. by executing `brew install tectonic`, it installs all the needed dependencies for compiling latex projects.
